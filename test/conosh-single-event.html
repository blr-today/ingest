About Savage:

The One Michelin-starred restaurant takes its inspiration from the unique background of Executive Chef, Andrea Selvaggini. Growing up on the outskirts of Rome, he was affectionately known as ''Selvaggio''. With culinary experience spanning Italy, France, Luxembourg, Spain, Mexico, and, of course, Norway, Andrea has left his mark on esteemed kitchen teams worldwide, including renowned establishments like Quique Dacosta, Quintonil, Maaemo and Kontrast.

Savage not only pays homage to Chef Andrea's journey but also symbolises the untamed nature that serves as the source of much of the kitchen's raw materials. Whether sourced from Norway's cold seas or its pristine forests, many elements originate deep in the wild before being skilfully crafted by the Savage kitchen team, showcasing the essence of nature's bounty.

About Chef Andrea Selvaggini:

Born in 1995, Andrea Selvaggini is a young chef with an impressive career who has worked in the most famous and important restaurants of the world today is the chef of Savage restaurant in Oslo, proudly holding 1 Michelin star. 

However, it all starts in his homeland - Italy, in the Lazio’s region city of Tarquinia, where Andrea was born and grew up, and where since his early childhood he was inspired by cooking, being surrounded by great Italian cuisine, and pushed forward thanks to his desire to travel the world. As a result, he was sure about his future: he wanted to unite two of his passions - cooking and travelling and make the job of it. Selvaggini decided to dedicate his life to cooking and, after completing the educational path at the culinary school he went straight to France and then Luxemburg, to gain his first professional experiences. But after working in some bistros in those countries, he discovered a strong passion towards the Fine Dining scene inside of him. That made a true breakthrough in his life. Selvaggini decided to get back to Italy and to work first in Metamorfosi restaurant (1* Michelin) which completely blew his mind in terms of creativity and style of cooking and made him understand that the Fine Dining sphere was the right dimension for him. Then, he also went to work at Pascucci al Porticciolo restaurant (1* Michelin). 

Fine Dining became not a part, but his entire life. Selvaggini continued to fulfil his desire to travel and after his Italian experiences, he headed to Spain, to work a side of one of the greatest Spanish chefs - Quique Dacosta in his 3 Michelin-starred restaurant in Denia. After completing that part of his professional journey, he moved even more far away and life brought him to Mexico, directly at Quintonil restaurant by chef Jorge Vallejo in Mexico City (listed n.9 in the world according to the list The World’s 50 Best Restaurants).

After, another radical change of scenery Selvaggini went to Norway to work at Maaemo restaurant, holding 3 Michelin stars, where he spent two years starting as chef de partie and then becoming sous chef and concluding his career at Maaemo as an R&D chef there. His second Norwegian experience happened to be in Kontrast(1*Michelin) restaurant and the last one, before changing his life completely, happened to be at Einer restaurant where he was the Head Chef for 2 years. 

During the fall season of 2022, Andrea became a part of Revier Hotel and opened a Savage restaurant inside of it. Since the beginning, Selvaggini took a role as Executive chef and led Savage to its first Michelin star right after 7 months of the opening of the place.
